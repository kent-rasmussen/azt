# Word Collection

## Prerequisites
- [General workshop prerequisites](../WORKSHOPS.md#Prerequisites)
- Android phone(s) for recording — one per recording team, multiple teams per language possible — with the
  **A-Z+T Collab** (server) and **A-Z+T Recorder** [apps](https://github.com/kent-rasmussen/azt/wiki/Apps) installed.

## Goals
- Set up collaboration: connect the computer and the team's phones to the same database (A-Z+T Collab, preferably over the local network *and* GitHub), and confirm that a recording made on one phone appears on the other after a sync. This lets several speakers record in parallel.
- Decide on citation forms for nouns and verbs, and use the same form through all the recording.
- **A-Z+T Recorder** (phone): 
  - select which glosses you want to see.
  - Work through the wordlist item by item:
    - press-talk-release, recording each word as you say it
    - If you don't like the picture, click the circle in the picture to select another option.
    - swipe right to get the next word. 

## Follow-up Action Items
- Finish collecting the Comparative African wordlist.
- Keep the phones syncing (A-Z+T Collab), so that everyone's recordings land in the one team database.
- Make sure your database is available to whatever consultant will be helping you.
- Once you're done collecting your recordings, run Bulk ASR on your wordlist (you may ask your consultant for help with this)
