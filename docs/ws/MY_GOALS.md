# My Goals

When I come to work at a workshop, I will bring specific agenda items that I try to fulfill everywhere I work.

## Philosophy training
- As an expert in writing system development, with a Doctorate in Linguistics, I have spent a lot of time thinking through the best way to do this work. So when I am involved in the project, I work to bring the following truths to bear (borrowing heavily from Kutch Lojenga 1996):
  - The language is owned by those that speak it, and this should be represented in the ownership and maintenance of data and analyses of that language. However much foreign expertise or finances may be necessary to a project, these resources are helping the language community run a project; investment of foreign expertise or finances is part of our service to the community for the Glory of God, NOT an implication of ownership of the language project.
  - Language speakers are competent to analyze their language, at least to some extent, and should be treated accordingly.
  - It is the responsibility of outside consultants
    - to facilitate the competency already present in a language community
    - to add technical competencies where reasonable:
      - everyone should be encouraged to operate the computer
      - everyone should be encouraged to understand what we are doing, and to ask questions.
    - to help maintain standards of sustainability and durability with regard to the storage of data and other international standards.
    
## Mentorship
- Many people with any training or interest in linguistics would like more.
- Many projects wait for personnel available to help them.
- So where someone is willing to work with the philosophy above, I am happy to bring them along, and to share what I can to whatever level they can receive.

## Text collection
- Any good language project depends on a basic understanding of the language
- Language documentation is best based on the broad collection of texts of various genres -- whatever the goal of your project is.
- The largest bottleneck to text collection by professional linguists is transcription. To this end:
  - We collect texts orally [Basic Oral Language Documentation, BOLD](https://www.sil.org/language-culture-documentation/bold)
  - We develop Automated Speech Recognition (ASR; machine transcription) AI models to facilitate the transcription of oral texts previously collected.
